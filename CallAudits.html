Case Study: Call Center QA App

This project is based on work I performed as a Data Analyst working at a high volume call center where there was a drastic need to overhaul the Quality Assurance process. Though the implementation differs slightly, the intent of this project is to showcase core skills used to overcome problems faced by the organization utilizing familiar technologies. The following is a summary with code snippits from this repo (WIP).

The Problem

At the time, the company was tech-immature and new to even having a dedicated analytics team, spreadsheets and emails served as the core tools underlying most business functions. The call center staffed somewhere around 600 dialers across 30 teams, with only 2-3 Quality Assurance agents responsible for auditing and providing feedback for call volumes in the range of millions per week. A standardized form existed with pass/fail categories, where QA would listen to a selection of calls submitted by team leaders and respond via email their findings and follow up with coaching.

The core issues I identified in the process are:

  • Lack of historical data tracking
  • Indefinite/nonstandard procedure for selecting calls
  • Low coverage due to small sample

In the process of redefining the auditing procedure, I sought to answer these questions for the business which were not being addressed by the existing process:

  • Who are our highest and lowest performers?
  • What is the impact of quality on our KPI's?
  • Where are our greatest areas of opportunity?
  • How is call quality changing over time?
  • How confident are we in our accuracy of rating quality?

The Solution

The core of the new process was to utilize the tools available to build out an automation which removed the heavy manual burden of call selection and tracking with excel files. In the final iteration, the stack consisted of:

  • MySQL: Existing enterprise data warehouse containing production and agent information
  • SQLite: Local database for writing sample and audit data
  • Python: ETL scripts and CLI for orchestration
  • PowerAutomate: Flows to load data into forms
  • PowerApps: Form frontend for audit completion
  • PowerBI: Visualization and analysis

The sample repo differs slightly from this implementation but the core workflow is the same: on a weekly basis, a balanced random sample based on business parameters (2 calls that transferred and 2 calls where the client hung up per agent from the prior week) was selected, a subset of which was randomly assigned to the QA agents (5 calls of each call end type for each leader). Both groups, leaders and QA agents, would then score their assigned calls based on the existing scorecard categories by a deadline, after which results were recorded into a local database. Finally, a view in the audit database was used for transformations and calculations to be fed into PowerBI and shared with the organization.

The application had to work around limitations, namely that we did not have engineering support or an environment to host and schedule the script. In the end, a single operator orchestrated the loading and generation of audits through a simple CLI and a config file was used to make swappable components (such as creating exemption lists and tracking changes in the QA agent availability if there were to be taken off assingment even if temporarily). In future iterations or an ideal scenario, something like Airflow would enable scheduled jobs and all the data would live in a hosted environment rather than living in local files.
